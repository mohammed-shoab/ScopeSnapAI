# Founders Poll — Annotated AC Photo in Homeowner PDF Report

**Quick question — needs 2-3 sentences of reasoning from each of you.**

---

## Context

When a technician does an inspection in SnapAI, they take photos of the AC unit and the app flags issues by severity (Red = Critical, Orange = Medium, Green = Minor).

**The question:** Should the homeowner PDF report include one of those inspection photos with the issues circled/marked directly on the image (red/orange/green circles), so the homeowner can visually see what's wrong before deciding on a repair option?

---

## The two sides

**For including it:**
- Homeowners trust what they can *see* — a photo with a circled capacitor is more compelling than "Capacitor – Weak" in text
- Reduces "is this real?" skepticism and could increase estimate acceptance rates
- Other inspection report apps (home inspection, car inspection) all do this

**Against including it:**
- Adds complexity — we need to auto-annotate photos with AI or let techs draw circles manually
- Photo quality from field conditions varies; a blurry circled photo might look unprofessional
- Some homeowners might get scared off by seeing the actual damage rather than a clean summary
